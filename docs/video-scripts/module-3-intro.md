# Module 3 Intro — Reports and Documents

- **Instructor:** Bobby
- **Target runtime:** ~75 seconds
- **Audience:** Cashman employees, brand new to AI
- **Goal:** Frame report writing as the next big time-saver after email, introduce the concept of *hallucinations*, and preview the three lessons.

## Script

Hey, I'm Bobby. Welcome to Module 3.

Let's talk about reports. Monthly progress reports, closeout summaries, RFI responses, daily logs. If you're like me, the writing part isn't the hard part -- it's the *starting* part. Staring at a blank page is brutal.

AI fixes that. You give it your raw notes -- bullet points, scribbles from a job walk, whatever you've got -- and it gives you back a structured first draft in about thirty seconds. Your job shifts from writing to **reviewing and refining**, which is way faster, and honestly, more fun.

We're going to cover three things.

First, **writing from scratch.** I'll show you how to feed AI a few facts about a project and get back a real, formal report you could actually send.

Second, **rewriting and editing.** AI is fantastic at taking a rambling, casual draft and tightening it up.

Third, **building reusable templates.** Once you've written a great report once, AI can help you turn it into a template you reuse every month -- so you don't reinvent the wheel.

I'm also going to teach you a really important concept called **hallucination**. That's when AI makes something up that sounds totally plausible -- a number, a date, a name -- but it's not actually true. We'll learn how to catch it. Because here's the rule: **whatever you send out, you're responsible for.** AI doesn't sign your name. You do.

Let's get into it.
