- **Target runtime:** ~80 seconds

- **Goal:** Show that AI now creates visual and audio content, preview the three lessons, and introduce the deepfake risk.

## Script

Bobby here for Module 5.

So far we've talked about AI as a writer. But AI does a lot more than write. It can **generate images** for your slides. It can **build presentations** from a few bullet points. It can **transcribe a meeting** and email out the action items before you're back at your desk.

That's what this module is about.

We'll do three exercises.

First, **creating images for presentations.** No graphic design skills required. You describe what you want -- "a wide shot of a marine construction site at sunrise, workers in PPE, professional photography style" -- and AI creates it.

Second, **building a slide deck.** We're going to take a project kickoff -- a real, $6 million bulkhead replacement -- and let AI draft a 10-slide deck. You'll see the workflow: ask for an outline first, then build it out slide by slide.

Third, **video, audio, and transcription.** Teams meetings, Zoom calls, voice memos -- AI can turn the audio into text, summarize the meeting, and even draft the follow-up email.

Now -- here's the part you really need to hear. The same technology that lets us generate cool images and clean transcripts also lets bad actors create **deepfakes**. Fake photos. Fake voicemails. There have been real cases of someone calling a finance manager with a perfect-sounding "CEO" voice asking for an urgent wire transfer. It was AI.

So: trust your tools. Verify your messages. We'll cover exactly how. Let's get started.
