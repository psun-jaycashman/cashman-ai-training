## Script

Bobby again. Welcome to Module 4.

Quick question: when you need a tricky Excel formula, what do you do? If you're like most people, you Google it, click on a forum from 2014, scroll past four ads, and then try to adapt some stranger's example to your spreadsheet.

Those days are over.

In this module, you're going to learn how to just *describe* what you want -- in normal English -- and let AI write the formula for you. "I need column F to say OVER BUDGET if column C is greater than column B." That's it. AI hands you the formula.

We're going to cover three things.

First, **AI-powered formulas.** Real Cashman-style examples: budget tracking, cost variance, status flags.

Second, **data analysis.** AI can suggest *how* to look at a dataset -- what to compare, what chart to build, what story the numbers might be telling. It's like having a junior analyst on call.

Third, **cleaning up messy data.** Equipment logs from three different superintendents, all in different formats? AI can normalize that into a clean table in seconds.

But here's the rule for this whole module: **always test it.** AI formulas usually *look* right, but a wrong cell reference or an inverted condition can quietly break a report. Plug in a number where you already know the answer, and make sure the formula gives you that answer.

Trust, but verify. Let's go.
